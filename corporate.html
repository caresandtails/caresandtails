Vision

Persona

Anna Lopez -  35 years old 

 They live in a house with a backyard, with their 2 kids and husband Paul Lopez, they have been together since 2009. They enjoy spending time outside with their kids and their dog Valentin (a 3-year-old Golden Retriever). During the day she works as a math university teacher and Paul works as an accountant, from 9 to 17. Their kids are 7 and 10 so they spend their days in school. During this time Valentin is alone at home, which is a hassle for the family. Anna and Paul try to make some time for themselves, so they go out with their friends once per week. Anna and her husband have an above average income, so she takes her family on vacation outside the country on holidays, having the trouble to leave Valentin behind. Anna and Paul live abroad from their families, so they usually can’t rely on them to take care of their dog.

Daniel di Angelo -  26 years old 

He lives alone in an apartment in the center of the city He works in finance in an office, from 8 to 17. He has an average income. He owns a small dog named Tofu (an 8-month-old white Pomeranian) He got Tofu when he moved to the capital for his new job, to have some company and comfort in his journey. He is from a small village in the outsides of the city. He is often busy with work but when he is free, he spends time at home with Tofu. He takes him on walks at night and before work. Daniel feels guilty about not being able to spend time with him and having to leave him alone due to work. On holidays he brings Tofu with him, but he must leave him at home when he goes on work trips, which is often.

Management

Lean Canvas

Fill in at least 2 bullet points per building block and according to the rubrics.

Problem

  • List your top 3 problems you solve for your target group(s).
  • Describe briefly how the problems are solved now (existing alternatives).

Solution

  • Outline the brand solution(s) you have for the above problem(s).

Customer segments

  • List your target customers and/or users.
  • Describe briefly the characteristics of your ideal customer (early adopter, brand persona).

Unique value proposition

  • With a single, clear compelling message, state why your brand idea is different and worth paying attention to.

Unfair advantage

  • List the aspect(s) of your brand that cannot easily be copied, also called your sustainable competitive advantage(s).

Channels

  • List your main path to customers.
  • How do you reach them? What channel(s) do you use.

Key metrics

  • List the key numbers that tell you how your brand experience is doing.
  • For example amount of users, downloads, visitors, subscriptions, sales etc. Numbers you are able to measure.

Revenue streams

  • List your sources of revenue: describe the revenue model and the (different) revenue stream(s).

Cost structure

  • List your main costs: define the fixed and variable costs.
  • Calculate the cost per unit.

Services/products

  • State what your product and/or service is and how this contributes to your unique value proposition.
  • Clarify the fit between the product/service developed and the brand identity/brand image.

Validation of Assumptions
